SOTP Valuation Overview

Our Sum-of-the-Parts (SOTP) valuation for PVI Holdings is anchored by its three distinct, high-performing pillars. The group's structure allows each entity to specialize while creating systemic synergies. The non-life insurance arm, PVI Insurance, represents the largest component of the group's operational value, complemented by the stable, high-growth reinsurance and asset management businesses.

Subsidiary Deep Dive: PVI Insurance

As the #1 non-life insurer in Vietnam, PVI Insurance is the group's flagship. Its diversified portfolio, strong market position, and leadership in industrial/commercial lines place it in a prime position to capture macro-driven growth.

GWP Portfolio Breakdown (est.)

Qualitative Growth Drivers

  • Health: Poised for significant expansion, driven by a rising middle class demanding higher-quality healthcare and corporate adoption of employee benefit packages. This segment benefits from low capital requirements and sticky customer relationships.
  • Property and Insurance: Directly correlated with Vietnam's surge in FDI and infrastructure development. Every new factory, industrial park, and commercial real estate project requires comprehensive property coverage, a core strength for PVI.
  • Hull and Liability Insurance: Growth is linked to Vietnam's status as a global manufacturing hub and its expanding export-oriented economy. Increased maritime trade and logistics complexity drive demand for both hull (vessel) and liability (cargo, operations) insurance.
  • Fire and Explosion: A compulsory insurance line for many businesses, its growth is tied to the formalization of the economy and stricter regulatory enforcement, particularly in new industrial and residential high-rise constructions.
  • Motorcycle/Motor: While the motorcycle segment is mature, the shift to higher-value automobiles, coupled with compulsory civil liability insurance, provides a stable, large-volume retail base and cross-selling opportunities.

Subsidiary Deep Dive: HanoiRe (PVI Re)

HanoiRe serves as a critical component of the domestic insurance ecosystem, capturing and managing risk as Vietnam's leading domestic reinsurer. Its growth is symbiotically linked to the primary insurance market's expansion.

Reinsurance Portfolio Breakdown (est.)

Qualitative Growth Drivers

  • Property: As the primary insurance market for property (driven by FDI and infrastructure) grows, the size and complexity of these risks increase. HanoiRe is essential for providing the capacity that allows primary insurers like PVI Insurance to underwrite these large-scale projects.
  • Aviation: Vietnam's aviation market is a key regional growth story. The post-pandemic recovery, fleet expansion by domestic carriers, and airport infrastructure upgrades all necessitate complex aviation reinsurance, creating a high-value, specialized growth niche for HanoiRe.

Subsidiary Deep Dive: PVI Asset Management (PVIAM)

PVIAM is the group's financial engine, managing the substantial "float" generated by the insurance businesses alongside a growing third-party asset portfolio. Its performance is linked to Vietnam's capital market development.

Assets Under Management (AUM)

Qualitative Growth Drivers

  • Managing Insurance Float: PVIAM's primary role is managing the ~20,000B VND float from PVI Insurance, a stable and growing capital base. Efficient management of this float generates stable investment income, a key part of the group's profitability.
  • Third-Party Fund Management: As Vietnam's capital markets mature and investor sophistication grows, PVIAM is well-positioned to attract more third-party assets, leveraging its track record and the PVI brand.
